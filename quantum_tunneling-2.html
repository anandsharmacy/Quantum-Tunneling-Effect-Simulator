Quantum Tunneling Effect Simulator

About Quantum Tunneling

Quantum tunneling is a quantum mechanical phenomenon where a particle passes through a potential energy barrier that classical physics would deem impenetrable. It arises because particles exhibit wave-like behavior governed by the Schrödinger equation — the wavefunction ψ has a non-zero amplitude on both sides of the barrier.

The simulation displays the real part ℜ(ψ) in red and the imaginary part ℑ(ψ) in dark blue, along with the potential V(x) in steel-blue.

Real-World Applications

Nuclear fusion in stars · Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM) · Tunnel diodes · Flash memory (NAND) · Enzyme catalysis · Alpha decay

About this Simulator

The TDSE is solved with the Crank–Nicolson implicit scheme — unconditionally stable, unitary (norm-preserving), and 2nd-order accurate in both Δx and Δt.

Equations Used

Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation

Governing equationiℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ = [−ℏ²/2m · ∂²/∂x² + V(x)] ψ

Crank–Nicolson Scheme

CN update (α = Δt/2ℏ)(I + iα·Ĥ) ψⁿ⁺¹ = (I − iα·Ĥ) ψⁿ

Initial Gaussian Wavepacket

ψ(x,0)ψ = (2πσ²)^(−1/4) · exp[−(x−x₀)²/4σ²] · e^(ik₀x)

Transmission & Reflection

ProbabilitiesT = ∫_{x>x_R} |ψ|² dx  |  R = ∫_{x<x_L} |ψ|² dx

WKB Estimate (rectangular barrier)

Tunneling probabilityT ≈ exp[−2L √(2m(V₀−E)) / ℏ]

How the Simulator Works

  1. Grid init: N = 700 uniform points over x ∈ [−160, 160], ℏ = m = 1.
  2. Potential V(x): Chosen barrier profile evaluated on the grid.
  3. Wavepacket: Normalised Gaussian ψ(x,0) with momentum k₀ and spread σ, placed at x₀ = −100.
  4. Hamiltonian: Kinetic energy via central differences → tridiagonal matrix H with V(x) on diagonal.
  5. CN matrices: A = I + iαH, B = I − iαH assembled; Thomas forward-sweep precomputed once.
  6. Time loop: (1) RHS = B·ψⁿ, (2) solve A·ψⁿ⁺¹ = RHS via Thomas O(N), (3) renormalise every 40 steps.
  7. Rendering: ℜ(ψ) red, ℑ(ψ) dark blue, V(x) steel-blue — matplotlib-style with grid, y-axis, and legend.
  8. Readouts: T and R computed by integrating |ψ|² over right/left domains in real time.
